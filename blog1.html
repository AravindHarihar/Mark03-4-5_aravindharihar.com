Civil Engineering to WebDev

Yes, you read that right!

I did my graduation in Civil Engineering but now I am learning web development.

What? Why? How? you will know in this blog.


Why I chose Civil Engineering?

I was really passionate about large buildings, bridges, dams, etc, and always wondered how they're built.
My interest gradually increased as I researched more and more about it. Moreover, I did not have enough marks in 12th to choose options like CS, EC, etc.

I thoroughly enjoyed my time in college and loved studying civil. So much so that, I was among the class toppers and in some subjects, I was among the top scorers in the entire University!


Luck or effort?

When I was in my final year, there were many IT companies that came for campus placements. I wanted to experience a corporate interview once in my life and also was getting free attendance if I went to the interview, so I thought, Why Not?


First I had to give the aptitude test and I cleared it easily. Then there was a HR round. I cleared this round too very easily. And guess what? I got the offer letter!


But it did not matter to me as I was very clear that I will NOT join any IT company. Why? because civil was love ❤️ .


What changed my mind?

During the final days of my college, I started a small startup with my friends. It was a matrimonial website for a very small community in Bangalore.

I took in-charge of the product and my friends took up different roles like marketing, social media, etc,

While the website was being developed I used to go to the developer often and used to discuss various ideas with him.
During this process, I started to understand the power of code.

The code he wrote, could support even 10,000 users every day, this made me think about the impact and reach of a website or an app. We could reach even a million people just by sitting in our rooms.

I started to understand that no matter what building I construct, I cannot have that level of impact . This whole process inclined me towards pursuing a career in this field.

So I decided to join the company in which I was placed.

My First Job

Once I joined the company, I was given training in languages like Java and Python. Guess what? I failed the Java exam.
I started to feel this isn't my cup of tea.


with a lot of effort, I was able to clear the exams. Then I was put into QA.


When I was in QA, I understood how products work end-to-end. This gave me a clear picture of how software is managed and how it is built from scratch. Over the period of 2 years I enjoyed this process and now I feel it is time to create something!

Create for myself, for clients, and for open source.

Code and media has high impact, and I want to make an impact on the world!

That's all folks!
